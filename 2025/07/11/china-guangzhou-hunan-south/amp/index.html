GUANGDONG – RURAL AREAS (Shantou, Zhanjiang)

CHINA DANXIA WHS
China Danxia is a landscape of red terrigenous sedimentary beds influenced by uplift, weathering and erosion. It comprises six areas (Mount Langshan and Mount Wanfo (Hunan Province), Mount Danxia (Guangdong Province), Taining and Mount Guanzhi (Fujian Province), Mount Longhu and Guifeng (Jiangxi Province), Fangyan, Mount Jianglang (Zhejiang Province), and Mount Chishui (Guizhou Province). It is in the sub-tropical zone of south-eastern China within a 1700 km crescent shaped arc from Guizhou Province in the west to Zhejiang Province in the east. They are characterized by spectacular red cliffs and erosional landforms, including dramatic natural pillars, towers, ravines, valleys and waterfalls.
Conserved sub-tropical broad-leaved evergreen forests, and host many species of flora and fauna, about 400 of which are considered rare or threatened.
Danxiashan. The Danxia landform is named after Mount Danxia, one of the most famous examples of this landform.
Get there. High speed train from Guangzhou to Shaoguan Station. Take bus line 22, 30-40 minutes to the bus terminal and the old train station (2 RMB). Take a bus 16 RMB or shared cabs
See. Many tourist buses to every point of interest. Yangyuan Stone or male stone, the large red phallus stone. Yingyuan Stone or women stone.
Zhanglao Peak or Elder Peak, cable car to the top of the peak – forest, springs, pavilion, monasteries and hiking trails. Hike down the peak to Biechuan Buddhist Monastery.
River cruise on Jinjiang River, the view along the river was breathtaking and highly recommended. Do not visit Danxiashan in summer, this area is really hot and uncomfortably humid,
Guifeng section of the Longhushan National Park. It is separate from the main Longhushan park. It is very compact. There is one main trail that everybody follows. 8.7 km in total, loop took me 3.5 hours with plenty of stops.
Danxia ‘inselbergs’. panoramic views of the landscape of isolated, eroded peaks. Dan Xia means “vermillion sunglow”.
The trail ends at the dock, from where a (heavily overpriced) ‘sightseeing’ boat will take you back to the visitor center. no other trail to walk out.
Guifeng is an easy half-day trip from Shangrao, which itself is a good hub for the WHS of Sanqingshan and Wuyishan. Take the fast train to the city of Yiyang from Shangrao (17mins) and from Yiyang railway station, take bus #2 or a Didi taxi (15mins/40 yuan) to the site, known as ‘Guifeng Scenic Area’ or ‘Guifeng Mountain’. I left Shangrao on the 7.30 train and got back on the 13.01 from Yiyang. The site entrance ticket costs 60 yuan (for some reason I did not have to pay this), plus 20 yuan for the shuttle ‘train’ from the visitor center to the start of the mountain trail and 80 yuan for the boat ride
Mount Wanfo
Taipingyan Village of Wanfo Mountain is located in Linkou Town, Tongdao Dong Autonomous County. It is the biggest scenic spot in Tongdao and one of the biggest Danxia landforms in China “the Great Green Wall”.
The 168 kilometer-long Danxia Landforms of Mount Wanfo Scenic Spot contains five big scenic areas: Mount Wanfo, Immortal Cave, Seven-star Hill, General Hill and Ziyun Hill. Inside the area, there are “ten wonderful sceneries”: Duyan Hill, Seven-star Ancient Temple, Luck Cave, Lion watching Moon, One Pillar Supporting the Sky, Lady Waiting for Her Husband, Whelk, Tortoise Looking for Food, Inborn Magpie Bridge and Thirty-six Curve Forest Maze. One must be fascinated by its unique beauty and miracle landscape.
Mt Guanzhi “Lotus Mountain,” is a sacred mountain for the local Hakka people in Liancheng, China. It’s known for its unique rock formations that resemble a lotus flower when viewed from afar. The area surrounding the mountain is characterized by fern plants, steep cliffs, and clear streams, which are home to the Chinese giant salamander.
Mount Jianglang (Zhejiang Province)
Three peaks from north to south make its recognizable “river-shaped” arrangement, as follows: Lang Feng, Ya Feng and Ling Feng. Lang Feng has elevation of 816.8 m (2,680 ft). In September 2013, Mount Jianglang was the scene for a stunt by Jeb Corliss where he flew between two of the peaks in a wingsuit, starting the jump from a helicopter.
